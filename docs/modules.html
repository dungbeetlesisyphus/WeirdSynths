Module Reference

Complete I/O documentation, parameters, and patch tips for every WeirdSynths module.

NERVE

20 HP · Face → CV

The core face-tracking module. Receives 468 MediaPipe face landmarks via UDP and converts facial gestures into calibrated CV signals. Each facial region (eyes, brows, jaw, mouth, head rotation) outputs independent voltages. Polyphonic output bundles all channels for one-cable routing.

WEBCAM → nerve_bridge.py → UDP:9000 → NERVE → CV outputs (0-10V / ±5V)

Patch Tips

Brow filter sweep: Connect L BROW → VCF cutoff input. Raise your eyebrow to open the filter. Use SMOOTH at ~40% to prevent jitter.
Jaw as VCA: Connect JAW → VCA level. Open your mouth to let sound through. Great for rhythmic gating.
Head pan: Route HEAD X to a stereo pan module. Tilt your head to pan the sound across the stereo field.

SKULL

12 HP · Face → Drums

A face-controlled drum machine. Blinks trigger kicks, jaw movements fire snares, eyebrow raises hit hi-hats. Built-in analog-modeled percussion synthesis means you don't need external sound sources — just patch the outputs to a mixer and start performing.

WEBCAM → nerve_bridge.py → UDP:9001 → SKULL → drum audio + triggers

Patch Tips

External processing: Take individual drum outputs (KICK, SNARE, HAT) to separate effects chains — reverb on snare, distortion on kick.
Trigger sequencing: Use trigger outputs (K TRG, S TRG, H TRG) to fire external drum modules or samplers for hybrid face+machine beats.
Sensitivity tuning: Start SENS around 60%. Too low = missed triggers. Too high = accidental fires from talking or micro-expressions.

MIRROR

14 HP · Face → Display

A dot-matrix CRT display that renders your face in green phosphor wireframe. Uses the same face landmark data as NERVE and SKULL, giving you visual feedback of what your face is doing while you perform. The display updates at ~30fps with phosphor persistence and scanline effects.

WEBCAM → nerve_bridge.py → UDP:9002 → MIRROR → CRT display

Patch Tips

Performance setup: Place MIRROR next to NERVE in your rack for instant visual feedback while performing. You'll see exactly what the tracking sees.
High persistence: Crank PERSISTENCE for a ghostly trail effect — your face leaves phosphor afterglow as you move.

VOICE

8 HP · Audio → CV

A real-time vocal analysis toolkit. Sing, hum, or play any monophonic audio source — VOICE extracts pitch (V/Oct), amplitude envelope, onset transients, spectral brightness, and harmonic content. The CRT display shows the detected note with a tuning indicator. No bridge required — works with any VCV audio input.

MIC / AUDIO IN → VOICE → V/Oct, Gate, Env, Onset, Brightness, Harmonics

Patch Tips

Sing to control a synth: V/OCT → oscillator pitch, GATE → VCA/envelope. Hum a melody and hear it played back through any synth voice.
Harmonic drone: HARM poly output → polyphonic oscillator. Sing one note, get a full harmonic series chord. Beautiful with detuned saws or FM voices.
Expressive filter: BRT → VCF cutoff. Your voice's brightness naturally opens and closes the filter. Whisper = dark, shout = bright.
Rhythmic triggering: ONST → clock input or envelope trigger. Percussive vocal sounds ("tch", "kk", "pff") become rhythmic triggers for sequencers.
